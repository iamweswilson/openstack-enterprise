OpenStack is not a cloud. It is not a project or a product. It is not a virtualization system or an API or a user interface or a set of standards. OpenStack is all of these things and more: it is a framework for doing IT infrastructure—all IT infrastructure—in as interchangeable and interoperable a way as we are ever likely to know how.
—Trevor Pott

Supporting your application on OpenStack will help you address new markets and customer segments. Future-proof your applications and be infrastructure-agnostic through OpenStack abstraction layers. OpenStack supports market-leading hypervisors, storage backends, and network subsystems, as well as x86, ARM, and Power architectures. OpenStack is deployed by users in every industry, for a variety of workloads, and supports ISVs with a wealth of community-provided resources and collaboration.

Cloud applications market is expected to become the primary reason that sustains the growth of the $200-billion enterprise applications space through the five-year forecast period…

—Albert Pang2

The OpenStack market is forecasted to reach $2.7B by 2017. We invite you to take advantage of the OpenStack community resources, such as education, testing, and technical expertise, to deliver your software on OpenStack. As Forrester asks, “OpenStack is Ready—Are You?”2

Thousands of users in various industries use OpenStack in production, including Comcast, Walmart, Best Buy, PayPal, AT&T and Bloomberg to name few.
